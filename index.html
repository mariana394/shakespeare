Conclusion

William Shakespeare was born on April 23, 1564, and on April 23, 1616, he died in his hometown of Stratford-upon-Avon at the age of fifty-two. We can say that Shakespeare is the father of the English language since 95% of the words he used in more than 37 plays and 154 sonnets are still used today, even though more than 400 years have passed! Shakespeare is one of the greatest contributors to modern English, introducing 1700 words used today and generating ways of expressing what we think in a simple way. Some of his most famous works are Romeo and Juliet, Macbeth, Hamlet, etc. Shakespeare is in our day to day without us realizing it, it can be related to the words we use, the music we listen to or the books we read. It's hard to believe that someone has so much influence in our life without us realizing it, cool isn't it?
