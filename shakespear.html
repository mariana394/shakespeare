William Shakespeare

BIOGRAPHY

William Shakespeare was born in April 1564 in Stratford-upon-Avon, England. His father, John, was a glove maker; and his mother, Mary, was the daughter of a farmer. William studied Latin, Greek, and history, but dropped out of school when he was 16 years old. Before he was 20 years old, he married Anne Hathaway. They had three children: Susana and twins Hamnet and Judith. Later he got a job as an actor, to later develop his writing skills. In 1593, the plague was a disease that killed thousands of people and the theaters were closed. During this time, Shakespeare wrote short poems called sonnets. He also helped build a new theater called The Globe, which opened in 1599 and had space for three thousand people. Finally, thanks to all his effort and dedication, William Shakespeare became rich and famous. He died at the age of 52, on April 23, 1616.
