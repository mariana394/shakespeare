Macbeth

Does the end justifies the means or do the means justify the end?

Play

Type of Work

Tragedy

Genre

England

Setting

1606

Year Published

PLOT

The story is about how Macbeth is persuaded by the prophecies of three witches to claim the royal crown and become king. But to do so, he needs to liquidate every single obstacle that held him back to fulfilling his destiny of reigning in the land of Scotland. Macbeth is blinded by all this ambition and commits monstrous acts which will make him gain enemies and a boycott to tear down his kingdom of tyranny. Being a story full of guilt, violence, power and betrayal.

Themes

Guilt:

  • Guilt is always present from the moment the King’s murder is committed, causing Macbeth and Lady Macbeth to hallucinate and lead them to commit more murders and in turn Lady goes mad and commits suicide.

Ambition

  • Macbeth’s defeat is due to his excessive ambition. Causing him to believe his power is threatened, beginning to murder his supposed enemies, causing him to be beheaded in battle.

Loyalty:

  • Loyalty and betrayal are a key point in the story, because from these actions the story unfolds, beginning with Macbeth’s loyalty to the king causing his title of Thane of Cawdor, to which he would later betray the king and his companion Banquo.

Fate and Free Will:

  • Macbeth when presenting herself with the witches and seeing the prophecies takes this as her destiny, as without performing any action the first prophecy is fulfilled. From this situation Macbeth decides to assassinate the king, which is a sign of his free will and consequently performs other actions that bring him closer to his death.

Message:

This story converse and endless topics that are still relevant as in today. Macbeth covers a point of view of how political encounters take place or how the search of power can blind our vision and take control of our actions, breaking all of our moral constraints. This story can be reflected alongside human history. In which people fight for power no matter the consequences.
